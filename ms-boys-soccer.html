Pre-Season · Week 1 of 3

Foundation Week

Establish culture, learn each other, and build the basics. Everyone starts fresh here — that's the point. High energy, short lines, lots of touches.

🏅 How Skill Differentiation Works

  • ◉ Beginner track — simplified version of every drill, more reps, more coaching attention
  • ◉ Advanced track — added constraints (fewer touches, speed, pressure, weak foot)
  • Small-sided games mix skill levels intentionally — more-experienced players must make teammates better

Every Session Structure

  • 0–5 min — Team Huddle: energy-setter, verse, chant
  • 5–15 min — Dynamic Warm-Up (ball at feet)
  • 15–40 min — Technical Focus (differentiated by level)
  • 40–55 min — Competitive Game (mixed teams)
  • 55–60 min — Cool-Down + Devotional Moment
Session 1 First Touch & Passing
Day 1 First Touch + Passing Gates ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
Bring it in tight. Introduce yourself and the team's 5 values: discipline, perseverance, sportsmanship, self-sacrifice, teamwork. Read 2 Timothy 2:4–5. Ask: "What does it mean to compete by the rules, for the One who enlisted you?" Take 2 quick answers. End with team chant — let them suggest one. First one wins for the season.
Set the tone: this team works hard because of who we represent, not just what we want to win.
5–15
Warm-Up: Dribble Maze Everyone
Every player has a ball in a 20×20 grid. Coach calls commands: Red light (sole stop), Green light (dribble), Blue (inside turn), Yellow (outside chop). Gradually increase speed. Ends with 1 minute of freestyle — show your best move.
This tells you everything. Watch who has natural comfort with the ball. Note the beginners — they'll need you near them in the next block.
15–35
Passing Gates (Differentiated)
Scatter 10 cone gates (2 cones, 2 yds apart) across the field. Pairs pass through as many gates as possible in 4 minutes. Track completions. Then beat your score.

◉ Beginner◉ Advanced
Distance5–8 yd passes, wide gates10–15 yds, narrow gates
Touch ruleUnlimited touches2 touches max (receive + pass)
FootDominant foot fineMust alternate feet each pass
ChallengeBeat your last scoreOne-touch through 3 gates in a row
Pair one beginner with one more experienced player. The experienced player practices leadership + weak foot. The beginner gets a mentor, not a lecture.
35–40
Rondo: 4v1 Everyone
Groups of 5 in a circle (~8 yds). 4 keep possession from 1 defender. Last passer when defender wins it goes in the middle. Played for 5 minutes. Great equalizer — even beginners can succeed with positioning.
Coach beginners to "show for the ball" (open body, in space). They don't need skill yet — they need positioning habits.
40–55
4v4 Passing Game Mixed Teams
30×20 yd grid. Score by passing through a cone gate (no shooting). Must complete 3 passes before scoring. Mix skill levels on each team. Rotate teams every 6 minutes.
Advanced add-on: If your team completes 5 passes in a row, next gate counts as 2 points. Encourages patience even for the strongest players.
55–60
Cool-Down Circle
Light quad and hamstring stretch. Go around the circle: each player says their name and one word for how they feel. Coach closes: "I saw effort from everyone today. That's what we're about." Optional: 10-second moment of silence as a team before the chant.
Day 2 Dribbling & 1v1 Moves ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
Quick accountability: "Who practiced juggling since yesterday?" (No shame if no one did — just plant the seed.) High energy — clap it up, get loud. Today's verse: Proverbs 27:17 — "As iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another." Frame: "Today we push each other to be better."
5–15
Warm-Up: Cone Slalom + Juggling Station Split
Half the team does a slalom course through 6 cones (20 yds), time themselves, try to beat their time. Other half does juggle challenge (count their best). Swap after 5 mins. Every player gets a number — encourage them to write it down and beat it by end of season.
15–35
Move of the Day: The Cut-Back Turn (Differentiated)
Everyone learns the same move: dribble toward a cone, inside foot chop back, accelerate away. Walk-through → slow → full speed. Then line drill attacking a standing coach.

◉ Beginner◉ Advanced
ApproachSlow dribble, wide setupSprint approach, tight space
DefenderCone only (stationary)Live defender (light pressure)
FinishNo finish required — just the turnTurn + shot on goal immediately
BonusTry with non-dominant foot onceMust finish with non-dominant foot
For beginners: slow it down. The move feels unnatural until it suddenly clicks. Celebrate that click loudly when it happens.
35–45
King of the Square Everyone
10×10 yd grids. Pairs: attacker tries to dribble across the defender's end line; defender tries to knock the ball out. 45 seconds per matchup. Rotate winners to new grids. Run simultaneously — everyone is playing at the same time, no standing in line.
Beginner tweak: Defender can only shuffle side-to-side (no tackling yet). Builds attacker confidence first.
45–55
5v5 Dribble-to-Score Game Mixed Teams
Goals replaced by end lines — score by dribbling over the line (no shooting). Rewards ball carriers. Advanced players challenged to use the cut-back in game; beginners just encouraged to drive forward with confidence.
55–60
Debrief + Verse Response
Calf stretch. "Who sharpened someone today?" — call out a teammate who made you better. Quick moment. Coach affirms: "That's a team. Let's go." Chant out.
Iron sharpens iron — the strongest players lifting everyone else is a kingdom principle, not just a soccer one.
Day 3 Shooting & Finishing ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
Energy check: "0–10, what's the team's intensity right now?" Whatever they say — challenge them to make it a 10 by the end. Verse: Philippians 4:13. "Everything we do today — with Him."
5–15
Pass-and-Move Warm-Up
Two lines at midfield cones. Pass across, follow your pass. After 3 minutes add: sprint to a wide cone after passing, then rejoin opposite line. Gets blood flowing with a ball involved.
15–35
Shooting Circuit (3 Stations, 5 min each)
Quick technique reminder: plant foot beside ball, strike with laces, follow through low. "Pick a corner — always have a target."

Station◉ Beginner◉ Advanced
① Straight shot12 yds, stationary ball, focus on technique18–20 yds, driven shot, pick corner
② Rolling ballCoach rolls slowly, player approaches and shootsFirst-time finish, no second touch allowed
③ Turn & shootReceive to feet, take a touch, then shootBack to goal, receive, spin, finish in one motion
Beginners: repetition over difficulty. 3 successful techniques beat 1 flashy attempt. Get the basics grooved.
35–45
3v1 into Goal Mixed
3 attackers (1 advanced + 2 beginners or all mixed) vs 1 defender + GK. Start at midfield. Emphasis: advanced player sets teammates up — not a solo show. Award bonus point if a beginner scores.
The advanced player being a facilitator teaches them the most valuable skill at this level: making others better. Coach it explicitly: "You're the engine. They're the finishers today."
45–55
First Full Scrimmage
Let them play. Minimal coaching. Watch. Take mental notes on natural positions, competitiveness, attitude. Who encourages? Who gets frustrated? Who tracks back? You're reading the team, not correcting it yet.
55–60
End of Week 1 Moment
Team circle. "What's one thing God is teaching you through being on this team?" (No pressure — just offer it.) Coach shares something too. High-five line. Chant out loud.
End-of-week moments build a culture that outlasts the season. Even the players who don't engage much are watching how this goes.
Pre-Season · Week 2 of 3

Defense, Shape & Transition

Now that everyone has had touches on the ball, teach the team how to win it back and how to strike fast when they do. Defense is how you compete — even with beginners.

⚡ Week 2 Focus: Competitive Edge

  • Beginners: learn where to be. Positioning is coachable — skill takes time.
  • Advanced: lead by example under pressure. Add constraints to keep them challenged.
  • Theme verse for the week: "Be strong and courageous." — Joshua 1:9
Session 4 Defensive Stance & Pressure
Day 4 Defending: Delay, Don't Dive ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
Joshua 1:9 — "Be strong and courageous." Frame it: "Defending takes courage. You're going to stand your ground today. That's not natural — but it's how good teams win." Chant to open.
5–15
Shadow Defensive Footwork
Pairs. One dribbles slowly. Partner stays in low defensive stance, 1 yd in front, mirroring. No tackling — just stay in front. Switch every 90 sec. Focus: toes, knees bent, goalside, eyes on the ball. Do it 3 rounds, then add: defender tries to force attacker left (their weak side).
For beginners: this is their moment. Defending is about positioning, not athleticism. They can be great at this fast.
15–35
1v1 Defending Drill (Differentiated)
Attacker starts 20 yds from goal, defender 5 yds away. Defender's only job: slow the attack, stay on feet, force to the weak side. Not to win the ball. 10 reps per pair.

◉ Beginner Defender◉ Advanced Defender
GoalStay in front for 5 secondsWin the ball cleanly without fouling
Attacker speedWalk/jog paceFull speed
SuccessNo lunge = successForce to weak side AND win ball
Loudly praise the patient defender. "That's exactly it — he made it hard without diving. That wins games."
35–45
Team Shape Walk-Through (No Ball)
Put everyone in your basic defensive formation. Walk through: pressure, cover, balance. Move them around by calling "ball goes left"... "ball switches"... "cross comes in." Like a chess drill. Do it 3× from different starting points. It will feel slow — that's fine. Muscle memory starts here.
45–55
5v5 Defensive Points Game
Score system: 1 pt for tackling and keeping possession, 2 pts for a GK save + distribution, 3 pts for a clean 2-minute defensive period, 1 pt for a goal. Changes the mindset from goals-only to total performance.
55–60
Cool-Down
Glute stretch, quad. Discuss: "What's the difference between courage and recklessness in defending?" One or two answers. Short prayer of thanks for the practice, then chant out.
Day 5 Transition & Counterattack ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
"How many goals are scored within 10 seconds of winning the ball? Most of them." Today is about being dangerous the moment you get possession. Hustle culture starts NOW.
5–15
Agility Warm-Up + Sprint Bursts
Cone patterns (or ladder if available): 2-feet-in, lateral shuffle, in-out. Then 4× 20 yd sprint at 80% — recovery walk back. Fast session needs primed legs.
15–35
Transition Game: Win It, Use It
3v3 in 25×20 grid, two small goals. Rule: when possession changes, the new team must get a shot within 6 seconds or possession resets. Teaches immediate forward thinking.

◉ Beginner◉ Advanced
Time limit8 seconds to shoot4 seconds to shoot
First actionJust advance the ball forwardMust switch direction before shooting
Advanced players: remind them the constraint exists so they can't just overpower beginners. They must be faster and smarter.
35–45
3v2 Counterattack Waves
3 attackers at midfield, 2 defenders at the 18. Coach passes, attackers go immediately. Emphasize: wide players stay wide, ball carrier commits the defender, pass early. After each rep, 2 attackers become defenders for the next wave.
Role assignment: Put beginners as wide runners — gives them clear, achievable roles (stay wide, run the channel). Advanced player plays #9, making decisions under pressure.
45–55
Scrimmage — Transition Focus
Normal scrimmage. Coach stops play maximum 3 times — only to highlight transition moments (good or missed). Keep the flow. Let them feel the speed.
55–60
Cool-Down
Hip flexors, calves. One word each: how did the team's hustle feel? Coach response to what they say. Chant out.
Day 6 Set Pieces + Team Identity ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle — Team Roles
Announce starting to think about positions. Ask players what position they want to play and why. Make notes. Verse: Romans 12:4–5 — "Just as each of us has one body with many members, and these members do not all have the same function..." Frame it: "Every role matters. The team needs everyone."
5–15
Warm-Up Rondo 5v2
5 keep-away vs 2 in the middle in a 12-yd circle. Two-touch max. Defenders win a point for every ball won in under 8 passes. Run 3 rounds of 2 minutes each. High energy.
15–30
Corner Kicks — 2 Plays
Play 1 (Near-post flick): One runner attacks near post, everyone else creates space. Simple. Put advanced player as corner taker.

Play 2 (Short corner): Ball to player at edge of box, cross from wider angle. Beginners learn runner routes; advanced learn delivery.

5 reps each, both sides. Defenders set up to create realistic pressure.
Keep it 2 plays. At this age, 5 plays = 0 plays. Two they own beats five they've heard.
30–45
Free Kicks + Wall Practice
Kicks from 20–25 yds. Teach wall setup: 3–4 players, GK covers far post. Rotate who takes kicks — don't only give it to the advanced players. Beginners get reps too, even if the execution isn't perfect. After 8 attempts, practice crossing free kicks with runners attacking.
Beginners taking free kicks in practice = confidence in games. The skill grows if they get reps.
45–55
Scrimmage w/ Set Piece Triggers
When a corner or free kick is earned, pause and run the actual play. Real-game reps in context.
55–60
End of Week 2 + Captain Vote
Stretch circle. Quietly hold up fingers to vote for team captain(s). Announce result. Let captain say something. Close with a prayer led by coach or volunteer player. Week 2 chant out.
Shared prayer, even brief, builds the kind of trust teams need to compete together under pressure.
Pre-Season · Week 3 of 3

Game-Ready Week

Put everything together. Simulate real game scenarios, establish your lineup, and build confidence heading into the season. Game week starts now.

🎯 Week 3 Goals

  • Beginners: know their position, know 1 set piece role, feel confident enough to compete
  • Advanced: lead on the field, communicate, take responsibility
  • Team: know your 3 principles. Run your 2 set pieces. Trust each other.
Session 7 Possession + Shape Under Pressure
Day 7 Possession & Playing Out of the Back ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
"Week 3. Game week is coming. Who's ready?" High energy call-and-response. Verse: 2 Timothy 1:7 — "For God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power, love, and self-discipline." "No panic-kicking. No fear. Today we play brave soccer."
5–15
Rondo 6v2 — Possession Warm-Up
6 keep-away vs 2 in 15×15 grid. Team of 6 earns a point every 10 consecutive passes. Two-touch limit for all. Changes every 3 minutes. Gets everyone's head up and passing sharp.
15–35
Building Out of the Back (Differentiated)
Half-field. GK + 3 defenders build against 2 high-press attackers. Goal: play the ball past the midfield line without hoofing it.

◉ Beginner Defenders◉ Advanced Defenders
Press speedAttackers press at 50% speedFull press, game speed
SuccessBall reaches midfield any wayMust maintain possession through midfield
GKCan hold 5 seconds before distributingMust distribute in 3 seconds
Praise the brave short pass under pressure every time. That's the behavior that wins games when it's 1–1 in the second half.
35–55
Full 7v7 or 8v8 Match Simulation
Use your formation and actual positions. Coach from the sideline only — no stepping onto the field. Allow 4–5 stoppages maximum. Focus stoppages only on your 3 team principles. Let them problem-solve in real time.
After each stoppage ask the players first: "What should we have done there?" Then reinforce the right answer.
55–60
Pre-Game Culture Setup
"What are our 5 team values?" — players say them (discipline, perseverance, sportsmanship, self-sacrifice, teamwork). "What are our 3 team principles on the field?" — players say them. Write them somewhere visible. Ownership is everything.
Day 8 GK + Defensive Shape + Full Scrimmage ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
Captain leads the huddle today. Coach steps back. Verse is captain's choice (gave them one to prep: Psalm 133:1 — "How good and pleasant it is when brothers dwell in unity"). Captain gives 1 minute of fire. You just watch.
Transferring leadership to the captain builds team ownership. The captain needs reps leading before game day.
5–15
Split: GK Work + Team Defensive Shape
GK (with assistant or coach helper): Diving saves low both sides (5 each), distribution (punt, throw, goal kick), command of cross. 2 players rotate through GK reps for development.

Outfield (you): 6 players in defensive shape, no ball. Call out "ball left," "switch," "cross coming" — unit shifts together. Walk-through → game pace. 3 rounds.
15–35
Compact Defending vs Attack (8v8)
8 vs 8 on 3/4 field. Defending team keeps shape and defends deep — goal is to not concede for 4 minutes. Attacking team gets possession and must break down a set block. Swap roles every 4 min. Advanced players play in key defensive roles; beginners fill in a set position and hold it.
35–55
Full Coached Scrimmage
Last big scrimmage before game week. 4–5 coaching stoppages max. Focus only on your top 2 corrections — not everything. Let them find solutions. GK rotates at halftime.
55–60
Every Player Shout-Out
Go around the circle — coach calls each player's name and says one specific strength they showed in the last 2 weeks. Every. Single. Player. Prepare this in advance. Don't wing it. This is powerful for the kids who aren't the stars.
Every player being seen and called by name — that's more than a coaching technique. It's ministry.
Day 9 Final Prep — Light, Sharp & Ready ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle — Game Week
High energy. "We have been preparing. We are ready. Whatever happens in that first game, we compete with everything we have — for each other and for God." Verse: 2 Timothy 2:4–5 again — full circle to Day 1. Chant.
5–15
Fun Warm-Up: Team Juggling World Cup
Everyone juggles. Drop = sit down (but keep counting from where you left off individually). Last one wins nothing — just pride. Coach tries too. Guaranteed laughs. Gets the energy right.
15–25
Set Piece Review — No Pressure
Walk through corners and free kicks. 3 reps each. No coaching — just reinforcing memory. If they do it wrong, just say "again" calmly. They know it by now.
25–48
Light Possession + Penalty Shootout
Rondo to stay sharp (8 min). Then fun penalty shootout — whole team, first side to 5 wins. GK gets reps too. Keep it loose, keep it fun. Fresh legs > extra work the day before a game.
Don't overwork them. A sharp, confident team beats a tired, over-drilled one. Protect the legs here.
48–60
Game Day Logistics + Team Moment
Cover: arrival time, what to bring, warm-up routine, starting lineup (if ready). Share it confidently — "everyone will play." Then: a full team prayer. Coach leads it. Thank God for health, for this team, for the chance to compete. Ask for courage, teamwork, and to represent the school well. Close with chant — the loudest of pre-season.
The team that prays together before a game remembers what the day is really about. It doesn't guarantee a win — it guarantees perspective.
Phase 2 — Game Season

In-Season Sessions

1–2 games + 1–2 training sessions per week. Pick the right session type based on where you are in the week. The goal: arrive at every game fresh, focused, and unified.

In-Season Weekly Framework

  • Day after game: Light or rest. No heavy training. Optional optional recovery touches.
  • 2+ days before a game: Use Session A (Fix) or Session C (Develop). Work harder here.
  • Day before a game: Session B only. Light, sharp, confident. No heavy legs.
  • Game day: Use the game-day warm-up every time — routine builds readiness.
In-Season Sessions Pick Based on Week
Session A Post-Game Fix (Address One Issue) ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle — Reset
After a loss: acknowledge it once, genuinely. "We didn't get the result. Here's the one thing we're going to fix today." After a win: "Good. Now let's be better." Never skip the huddle. The tone you set here shapes the hour. Remember the standard: we play to win, but our preparation, effort, and attitude — not the scoreboard — are what we're actually judged on (see "Win Like a Warrior, Lose Like a Warrior" in the Culture & Faith tab).
How a team handles adversity is formed in these moments — not in the game. After a hard loss, a coach who stays steady and focused on growth teaches something that outlasts the season.
5–15
High-Energy Rondo Warm-Up
5v2, then 6v2. Fast pace. Gets energy up. No dwelling on the last game — get them moving and competing immediately.
15–40
The One Fix — Choose Based on Last Game
Problem: Gave up goals from wide areas
→ 2 defenders close down wide attacker, one blocks cross, one tracks runner. Repeat 10× each side. Include beginners as the tracking runner — clear role.

Problem: Can't hold possession
→ 4v4 possession in 20×20, no goals. 5 passes = 1 point. 2-touch max for advanced, unlimited for beginners. Teaches scanning and movement.

Problem: Not creating chances
→ Wall pass + combination drill. 4 attackers vs 2 defenders, finish on goal. Advanced players must play beginners in before shooting.

Problem: Getting outworked (fitness/intensity)
→ High-intensity 3v3 games, 90-second rounds, no rest between rounds. Winner stays. Competitive pressure = fitness.
Name the problem at the start. Name it clearly. "We gave up 3 goals from crosses. Today we fix that." Boys this age respond well to direct clarity — they want to know the problem is being taken seriously.
40–55
Free Scrimmage
No stops. Let them play. Watch for the fix being applied naturally. Reward it verbally in real time.
55–60
Wrap-Up
"Did anyone use what we drilled today?" Take 2–3 answers. Close with a quick verse or encouragement. Chant out.
Session B Pre-Game Prep (Day Before) ▾
0–5
Huddle — Game Eve Energy
Build anticipation. "Tomorrow is our chance." Share the lineup. Address the opponent briefly: "They're competitive. So are we. Here's what matters: our shape and our effort." Keep it 2 points max.
5–15
Light Dynamic Warm-Up
High knees, side shuffles, butt kicks, leg swings. Moderate pace. No intensity yet — this is activation, not conditioning.
15–25
Set Piece Refresher
Corners and free kicks — 3 reps each, no pressure. Reinforce memory. Calm, confident repetition only.
25–50
Possession + Finishing — Light & Fun
Rondo to stay sharp (10 min). Then relaxed finishing — everyone takes 5 shots from their preferred spot. End with penalty competition: full squad, winner earns locker room bragging rights. Keep it FUN. Confidence goes into tomorrow's game.
Energy here should be 7/10. Not flat — but not gassed. The game tomorrow is what matters.
50–60
Game Plan + Team Prayer
Review: arrival time, warm-up plan, 2 tactical focuses. Captain speaks. Coach prays. Chant out — the loudest of the week.
Session C Midseason Development (Skill Growth) ▾
0–5
Opening Huddle
Midseason check-in. "Where are we? Where do we want to be? Who has gotten better since Day 1?" Celebrate growth out loud. Verse: Hebrews 12:1 — "Run with perseverance the race set before us."
5–15
Technical Warm-Up + Juggle Check
Ball at feet: inside/outside rolls, v-pull, toe taps. Then 2-minute juggle check — players beat their Day 1 number. Celebrate anyone who has improved.
15–40
New Move of the Month (Differentiated)
Month 1: Step-Over — fake one direction, burst the other
Month 2: Cruyff Turn — fake cross, drag ball back, turn away
Month 3: Outside foot pass + overlap combination

Teach → slow reps → full speed → apply in 2v1

◉ Beginner◉ Advanced
PracticeStationary cone as defender, unlimited repsLive defender, full speed, finish on goal
GoalGet the motion rightUse it 3× in the scrimmage — they announce it when they do
Advanced players announcing when they successfully use the move in the scrimmage = accountability + pride. They'll work harder to pull it off.
40–55
Position Rotation Scrimmage
Put players in unfamiliar spots. Defenders play striker. Midfielders defend. Builds empathy, develops versatility, and keeps it fresh midseason. Normal small-sided game with full energy.
55–60
Cool-Down — Player-Led Stretch
Rotate who leads the stretch each session. Today's stretch leader picks a teammate to give a genuine compliment to. Builds leadership and culture simultaneously.
Game Day Pre-Game Warm-Up Routine ▾

Do This the Same Way Every Game

  • Routine = readiness. When the warm-up is the same, nerves settle and focus comes naturally.
  • Captain runs it. You observe and adjust only if needed.
−30
Arrive Together — Team Jog
2 laps of the field as a team. Together. Sets collective focus immediately. No phones, no goofing off — helmets on from this moment.
−25
Dynamic Stretch (Team-Led)
High knees, butt kicks, hip circles, side shuffles, leg swings. Captain leads. 5 minutes. Coach is silent.
−20
Passing Pairs + Rondo
Quick passing pairs to get touches. Then 2-minute rondo to engage the mind. Eyes up, quick decisions.
−10
GK Warm-Up + Shape Walk-Through
GK takes 10 saves and 3 distributions. Outfield: one kickoff position walk-through and first defensive shape. Quick and calm.
−4
Pre-Game Talk + Prayer
2 tactical points max. "Today we _____ and _____." Captain speaks briefly. Coach prays — thank God, ask for safety, courage, unity. Then chant. Then go.
Short talks win. Long pre-game speeches lose the room. Two clear points + prayer + chant = locked in.
Culture & Faith Framework

Building a Team That Lasts

Xs and Os are what you teach. Culture is what they remember. Here's how to build both intentionally as the Covenant Classical Warriors.

"No soldier gets entangled in civilian pursuits, since his aim is to please the one who enlisted him. An athlete is not crowned unless he competes according to the rules."
2 Timothy 2:4–5 — Season Anchor Verse
Culture Our 5 Team Values ▾
①
Discipline
Doing the right thing even when no one's watching and even when you don't feel like it — showing up on time, finishing every rep, holding your defensive shape when you're tired. Discipline in practice is what shows up in the 80th minute of a game.
②
Perseverance
Sticking with a hard drill, a losing scrimmage, or a frustrating skill until it clicks. No quitting on a rep, a teammate, or a game. Praise the player who keeps battling after a mistake more than the player who never makes one.
③
Sportsmanship
Play to win — competing hard matters. But winning isn't the ultimate goal. Preparation, effort, and attitude are what we control. Win with grace. Lose with grace. Shake every hand, every time, regardless of the score.
④
Self-Sacrifice
The pass instead of the shot. The wide run instead of the show. Playing out of position because the team needs it. Self-sacrifice on the field is practice for self-sacrifice everywhere else in life.
⑤
Teamwork
The most important value of all. Help your teammate, and the team is helped — and when the team is helped, every player on it is helped too. Assists matter as much as goals. We track and call out assists out loud, every game.
Culture Assists Over Glory — The Warriors Way ▾
🏆
Why "assists" is our word
Some of the most successful teams aren't the ones that score the most — they're the ones that lead in assists. Winning is the result of helping a teammate, not chasing individual glory. We are Warriors. Assists are how Warriors play.
→
How to coach it
Call out assists as loud as goals — in practice scrimmages and real games. Ask after every match: "Who had the best assist today?" before "Who scored?" Tell your most skilled players directly: your job is to make the player next to you better. That's the standard, not just scoring the most.
Culture Win Like a Warrior, Lose Like a Warrior ▾
🤝
The standard for both
We play to win — competing hard matters. But preparation, effort, and attitude — not the scoreboard — are what we actually control and what we're judged on. That means: shake the other team's hand the same way whether you won by five or lost by five. No celebrating in an opponent's face. No sulking, blaming, or excuse-making after a loss.
→
Use this after a tough loss
Sometimes a team that just got beat hasn't yet learned how to lose well — and sometimes a team that just won big hasn't learned how to win graciously either. Both are coachable moments. Name it plainly in the huddle: "We learn more from how we just handled that than from the final score." Then move to fixing one specific thing (see Session A in the In-Season tab).

Inclusion Managing Mixed Skill Levels Gracefully ▾
🔑
Never rank players publicly
Beginners know they're beginners. They don't need you to confirm it. Frame differentiated drills as "challenges" — the harder version is the advanced challenge, not the default. Every player is working on their own target.
🔑
Private correction, public praise
Correct technique privately (pull them aside or crouch down to their level). Praise publicly and specifically: not "good job" but "Marcus, that first touch was exactly right — you opened your body before the ball arrived."
🔑
Give beginners meaningful roles
In games: every beginner has a specific job (e.g., "your only job is to be wide and available"). In drills: give them a version they can succeed at. Success builds confidence — confidence builds skill. Shame does not.
🔑
This is mentorship, not just pairing
Coaches mentoring players is one of the most valuable parts of being on a team — and the same principle works player-to-player. When an advanced player partners with a beginner all season, frame it out loud as mentorship: "You're not just playing next to him, you're responsible for helping him get better." That language elevates it from a drill mechanic to a real leadership role they can take pride in.
🔑
Advanced players earn their status by serving
Tell your best players directly: "Your job in practice is to make your teammates better. That's how you earn the right to be the difference-maker in games." This changes the dynamic from individual showboating to team building.
🔑
Playing time philosophy
At this age and this school context: everyone plays meaningful minutes. Not necessarily equal — you still want to compete — but no one should feel invisible. Set a minimum per player per game and honor it. Communicate it to parents early.
Every player is made in the image of God and chosen for this team for a reason. They deserve to feel that.
Devotions 3-Minute End-of-Practice Devotional Ideas ▾
①
The "Iron Sharpens Iron" Ask
"Who made someone better today? Call out a teammate." Go around — at least 3–4 players share. Ends on encouragement and builds the habit of seeing each other.
②
Gratitude Round
"One thing you're grateful for that has nothing to do with soccer." Quick go-around. Humanizes players to each other. Especially powerful after a tough loss.
③
Verse Response
Read the week's verse. Ask: "What did you do today that matched this?" or "Where did you fall short?" 2–3 answers. Coach responds honestly too.
④
Silent 10 Seconds
At the end of a big win or hard loss — coach says "10 seconds of silence as a team." No chant. Just stillness. Then chant. It signals that something bigger than the result just happened.

Tactics Formation & 3 Team Principles ▾
Form.
Recommended Formations
7v7: 1-2-3-1 — two defenders, three midfielders, one striker. Clear roles, easy to teach.
9v9: 1-3-2-3 — three defenders, two holding midfielders, three up front.
11v11: 1-4-3-3 — classic, balanced, plays to athletic midfielders.
Put your best athlete at defensive midfielder (not striker). They influence every possession and cover for defensive errors. It's the highest-impact position for a mixed-ability team.
①
Press high immediately after losing possession
The 3–4 seconds after you lose the ball is when you can win it back cheapest. Everyone presses toward the ball — not just forwards. Drill this until it's instinct.
②
Play wide and cross
At this age, defenses collapse toward center. Wide channels are almost always open. Train your wide players to receive and cross; train your center players to time runs. Simple but effective against any team.
③
Two defenders always stay behind the ball
No matter what. Even on corners. Even when you're losing. Prevents the catastrophic counterattack. Drill it at the start of every scrimmage until it's automatic.
Fitness Conditioning Philosophy ▾
→
Condition through the ball, not laps
Intensive rondos, transition drills, and high-pressure small-sided games build soccer-specific fitness without boring running. 12–14 year olds disengage fast from pure cardio. Keep a ball involved at all times.
→
Watch for fatigue vs. effort
If players jog through a scrimmage, add a constraint: "Can't score unless every outfield player has touched the ball this possession." Raises intensity without adding physical work.
→
Pre-game legs
No intense training day before a game. The session before a game is for sharpness and confidence — not fitness gains. Overtrained 13-year-olds play flat.
→
Hydration reminder every session
Build in a water break around the 20-minute mark and at the transition to the game. Middle schoolers often don't self-regulate hydration well. It affects performance and mood.
Halftime Halftime Script ▾
1 min
Water, breathe, settle
Don't talk while they're still catching their breath. Let them drink and calm down first. You observe and organize your thoughts.
1 min
One thing we did well
Name it specifically. "Our press in the first 10 minutes was excellent." Not generic — specific. They need to know what to keep doing.
1 min
One adjustment
One thing only. "Their #7 keeps running in behind our left back. Left back, shift back 5 steps when the ball is on their right side." Specific, actionable, one player or one zone.
30 sec
Captain fires them up
Captain gets the last word. Coach steps back. Chant. Go.
Total halftime: 3.5 minutes max. Long halftimes lose the room. Short, clear, and fired up wins every time at this age.

Pro tip
Pre-Set the Field
Arrive 10 minutes early and set your grids before players show up. Mark a standard 20×20 and 30×20 that you reuse all season. Dead setup time kills momentum and loses middle schoolers immediately.
Nice
Coaching Addition
Small whiteboard or notepad for halftime/team talks. Speeds up showing formations. Also: a way to track each player's juggling count and a "move they're working on" — review it monthly.
